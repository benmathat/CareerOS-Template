# Status Definitions & Transition Rules

This document standardizes status values across all tracking documents in the Career workspace. Use these definitions to ensure consistency and clarity.

## Application Status Values

### Active Statuses (In Progress)

#### Identified
**Definition:** You've found an interesting opportunity but haven't applied yet.

**When to use:**
- Job posting saved for evaluation
- Recruiter reached out, initial conversation pending
- Company identified as target, no specific role yet

**Typical duration:** 1-7 days (should move to Applied or archive)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Applied (you decide to apply)
- → On Hold (need more information)
- → Withdrawn (decide not to pursue)

---

#### Applied
**Definition:** Application has been submitted.

**When to use:**
- Resume and cover letter submitted through ATS
- Application form completed
- Referral submitted

**Typical duration:** 1-3 weeks (waiting for response)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Recruiter Screen (moved forward)
- → Rejected (not selected)
- → On Hold (no response after 2+ weeks)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Confirm application was received (if possible)
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder for 1-2 weeks
- [ ] Update application tracker with date applied

---

#### Recruiter Screen
**Definition:** Initial screening call with recruiter or talent acquisition.

**When to use:**
- Phone/video call scheduled or completed with recruiter
- Initial qualification conversation

**Typical duration:** 1-3 days (call happens quickly after application)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Hiring Manager (passed screen, moving forward)
- → Rejected (didn't pass screen)
- → On Hold (recruiter needs to check with team)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Prepare for call (review role, company, your background)
- [ ] Send thank-you note after call
- [ ] Follow up if no response in 1 week

---

#### Hiring Manager
**Definition:** Interview with the hiring manager or direct supervisor.

**When to use:**
- Interview scheduled or completed with hiring manager
- First round interview (if recruiter screen was separate)

**Typical duration:** 3-7 days (scheduling and completion)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Panel / Onsite (moving to next round)
- → Final (skipping to final round)
- → Rejected (not moving forward)
- → On Hold (hiring manager needs time to decide)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Deep research on role and company
- [ ] Prepare STAR/SOAR stories relevant to role
- [ ] Prepare questions about role, team, and company
- [ ] Send thank-you note within 24 hours
- [ ] Follow up in 1 week if no response

---

#### Panel / Onsite
**Definition:** Multi-person interview, technical assessment, or onsite visit.

**When to use:**
- Panel interview scheduled or completed
- Technical/coding interview
- Onsite visit (in-person or virtual)
- Multiple interviews in same round

**Typical duration:** 1-2 weeks (scheduling multiple people takes time)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Final (moving to final round)
- → Offer (skipping final, going straight to offer)
- → Rejected (not moving forward)
- → On Hold (team needs time to debrief)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Research all interviewers on LinkedIn
- [ ] Prepare for technical/role-specific assessments
- [ ] Deep company research (culture, recent news, challenges)
- [ ] Prepare questions for each interviewer
- [ ] Send thank-you notes to all interviewers within 24 hours
- [ ] Follow up in 1 week if no response

---

#### Final
**Definition:** Final round interview, typically with senior leadership or executive team.

**When to use:**
- Final interview scheduled or completed
- Executive interview
- Last round before decision

**Typical duration:** 3-7 days

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Offer (selected!)
- → Rejected (not selected)
- → On Hold (final decision pending)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Prepare for executive-level questions
- [ ] Research compensation and prepare negotiation strategy
- [ ] Prepare questions about company vision and strategy
- [ ] Send thank-you note within 24 hours
- [ ] Follow up in 1 week if no response
- [ ] Prepare for offer negotiation (research market rates, identify priorities)

---

#### Offer
**Definition:** Offer received and under consideration.

**When to use:**
- Verbal or written offer received
- Negotiation in progress
- Offer accepted (keep status until start date)

**Typical duration:** 1-2 weeks (negotiation and decision)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Withdrawn (you decline the offer)
- → Archive (offer accepted, move to archive)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Review offer details carefully
- [ ] Research market compensation for role/location
- [ ] Prepare negotiation points (if applicable)
- [ ] Evaluate against your criteria (role targets, preferences, non-negotiables)
- [ ] Use decision matrix in application detail file
- [ ] Respond within agreed timeframe

---

### Inactive Statuses (Not Moving Forward)

#### On Hold
**Definition:** Process is paused, waiting for external factor.

**When to use:**
- Company paused hiring
- Waiting for budget approval
- Hiring manager on vacation
- Role being redefined
- You're waiting for more information

**Typical duration:** 1-4 weeks (should resolve or move to another status)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Any active status (process resumes)
- → Withdrawn (you decide to stop waiting)
- → Rejected (company decides not to proceed)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Set follow-up date (check in after 2 weeks)
- [ ] Document reason for hold
- [ ] Consider if worth continuing to wait
- [ ] Follow up with contact person
- [ ] If > 2 weeks with no update, consider withdrawing

---

#### Rejected
**Definition:** Not selected, process ended.

**When to use:**
- Explicit rejection received
- No response after multiple follow-ups (treat as implicit rejection)
- Role filled by someone else

**Typical duration:** Final status (archive after reflection)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Archive (after capturing learnings)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Capture feedback if provided
- [ ] Document in application detail file
- [ ] Update story inventory if gaps identified
- [ ] Add to coaching/feedback folder if useful
- [ ] Reflect on what worked and what didn't
- [ ] Archive application after reflection complete

---

#### Withdrawn
**Definition:** You decided to stop pursuing this opportunity.

**When to use:**
- You accepted another offer
- Role no longer aligns with your goals
- Company/role red flags discovered
- Better opportunity emerged
- Process taking too long

**Typical duration:** Final status (archive after documenting reason)

**Next possible statuses:**
- → Archive (after documenting reason)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Document reason for withdrawal
- [ ] Send professional withdrawal message (if appropriate)
- [ ] Update application tracker
- [ ] Archive application

---

## Status Transition Rules

### Normal Flow (Typical Progression)

```
Identified → Applied → Recruiter Screen → Hiring Manager → Panel/Onsite → Final → Offer
```

### Common Variations

**Fast track (small companies):**
```
Applied → Hiring Manager → Offer
```

**Skipping rounds:**
```
Applied → Recruiter Screen → Final → Offer
Applied → Hiring Manager → Offer
```

**Multiple rounds:**
```
Applied → Recruiter Screen → Hiring Manager → Panel → Panel → Final → Offer
```

### Exit Paths (Any Status Can Lead To)

- **Rejected** (from any active status)
- **Withdrawn** (from any status)
- **On Hold** (from any active status, then resumes or exits)

## Status-Based Action Items

### Quick Reference: What to Do at Each Status

| Status | Immediate Actions | Follow-Up Actions | Timeline |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Identified** | Research company, evaluate fit | Decide: Apply or archive | 1-7 days |
| **Applied** | Confirm receipt, log in tracker | Follow up in 1-2 weeks | 1-3 weeks |
| **Recruiter Screen** | Prepare for call, review role | Send thank-you, follow up in 1 week | 1-3 days |
| **Hiring Manager** | Deep research, prepare stories | Thank-you note, follow up in 1 week | 3-7 days |
| **Panel/Onsite** | Research interviewers, prep | Thank-you notes, follow up in 1 week | 1-2 weeks |
| **Final** | Executive prep, comp research | Thank-you note, prepare negotiation | 3-7 days |
| **Offer** | Review offer, research comp | Negotiate if needed, decide | 1-2 weeks |
| **On Hold** | Document reason, set follow-up | Check in after 2 weeks | 1-4 weeks |
| **Rejected** | Capture feedback, reflect | Update learnings, archive | Final |
| **Withdrawn** | Document reason | Send withdrawal message, archive | Final |

## Search Phase Statuses

### Current Phase (in `search-status.md`)

These are different from application statuses - they describe your overall search state:

- **Exploration** - Researching roles, companies, and opportunities
- **Active Applications** - Actively applying to roles
- **Interviewing** - In active interview processes
- **Offer Evaluation** - Evaluating one or more offers
- **Paused / Maintenance** - Search on hold, maintaining system

**Phase transitions:**
- Exploration → Active Applications (starting to apply)
- Active Applications → Interviewing (getting interviews)
- Interviewing → Offer Evaluation (received offers)
- Any phase → Paused / Maintenance (taking a break)
- Paused / Maintenance → Any phase (resuming search)

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Update Status

1. **Immediately** when status changes (don't wait)
2. **After each interview** (update to next status)
3. **After receiving communication** (offer, rejection, etc.)
4. **Weekly review** (check for stale statuses)

### Status Hygiene

- **Don't let statuses get stale** - If "Applied" for >3 weeks with no response, consider "On Hold" or "Rejected"
- **Be honest** - Use "Rejected" if you haven't heard back after multiple follow-ups
- **Archive promptly** - Move closed applications (Rejected, Withdrawn, accepted Offer) to archive after reflection

### Multiple Applications to Same Company

If applying to multiple roles at the same company:
- Create separate application detail files
- Track separately in application tracker
- Use same status values for each

For visual tracking (if using markdown viewers that support HTML or color-coding):

- 🟢 **Green (Active/Positive):** Applied, Recruiter Screen, Hiring Manager, Panel/Onsite, Final, Offer
- 🟡 **Yellow (In Progress/Uncertain):** Identified, On Hold
- 🔴 **Red (Closed/Negative):** Rejected, Withdrawn

## Notes

- These statuses are **guidelines, not rigid rules** - adapt as needed for your situation
- **Consistency matters more than perfection** - pick a status and stick with it
- **Update statuses regularly** - stale statuses reduce the value of tracking
- **Use statuses to drive action** - each status has associated next steps
