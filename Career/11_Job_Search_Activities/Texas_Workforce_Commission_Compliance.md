# Texas Workforce Commission (TWC)
## Unemployment Insurance Compliance Guide

**Purpose**  
This document provides a comprehensive, practical guide for achieving and maintaining full compliance with unemployment insurance requirements enforced by the Texas Workforce Commission (TWC).

It is written to be:
- Audit-ready
- Low-friction to maintain
- Defensible in the event of disputes, investigations, or appeals

This guide should be treated as a **compliance reference and operating manual**, not merely informational material.

---

## 1. Core Compliance Obligations

TWC unemployment compliance rests on four foundational obligations that must be met **every benefit week**.

### 1.1 Weekly Eligibility
To remain eligible, you must:
- Be able to work
- Be available for work
- Be actively seeking work
- Not refuse suitable work

Eligibility is assessed **weekly**, not just at the start of your claim.

---

### 1.2 Work Search Requirement
Each claimant is assigned a minimum number of work search activities per week.

Key points:
- The required number can change
- Notifications may appear in:
  - TWC correspondence
  - Your online claim portal
  - Workforce Solutions communications
- You are responsible for knowing your current requirement, even if it changes

Failure to meet the required number in any week can result in denial for that week.

---

### 1.3 Weekly Certification
For every benefit week, you must submit a certification affirming that:
- You completed the required number of work search activities
- You earned no disqualifying income
- You were available and able to work

Missed or incorrect certifications are the most common cause of payment delays and denials.

---

### 1.4 Recordkeeping & Audit Readiness
You are not required to submit work search logs weekly, but TWC may request them at any time.

You must be able to:
- Produce records promptly
- Demonstrate consistency and accuracy
- Show good-faith effort to obtain employment

Your records may be reviewed weeks or months after the fact.

---

## 2. What Counts as a Valid Work Search Activity (Texas)

TWC considers any **reasonable, verifiable action intended to obtain employment** to be a valid work search activity.

### 2.1 Commonly Accepted Activities
- Submitting job applications
- Participating in interviews (phone, video, or in person)
- Contacting recruiters or hiring managers
- Networking with professional contacts
- Attending job fairs or hiring events
- Registering with job boards or staffing agencies
- Customizing résumés or cover letters for specific roles

---

### 2.2 Activities That Often Fail Review
The following are frequently rejected **when logged without detail**:
- “Looked online”
- “Job search”
- “Checked postings”
- “Updated résumé” (without a specific role or employer)

These activities must be clearly tied to a concrete employment opportunity.

---

## 3. Required Documentation Elements

For each work search activity, you should be able to provide the following:

| Element | Purpose |
|------|------|
| Date | Confirms weekly compliance |
| Activity type | Confirms legitimacy |
| Employer or platform | Enables verification |
| Position or focus | Demonstrates suitability |
| Method | Shows level of effort |
| Outcome | Demonstrates good faith |
| Contact information | Allows follow-up if needed |

If a reasonable third party could verify that the activity occurred, it is typically sufficient.

---

## 4. Logging Best Practices (Gold Standard)

### 4.1 Maintain One Canonical Log
Use a single authoritative log that:
- Is updated daily
- Is grouped by benefit week
- Is not retroactively altered without notation

This log is your primary compliance artifact.

---

### 4.2 Weekly Certification Snapshot
Each week, record:
- Total number of activities completed
- Minimum required activities
- Certification submission date

This protects you against:
- System errors
- Disputes
- Delayed investigations

---

### 4.3 Evidence Retention
While not mandatory for every entry, retain evidence where available:
- Application confirmations
- Emails
- Interview invitations
- Calendar events

Evidence should be organized and referenceable.

---

## 5. Recommended Weekly Workflow

### Daily (2–5 minutes)
1. Perform a work search activity
2. Log it immediately
3. Save any available proof

### Weekly (10 minutes)
1. Count activities
2. Verify the requirement is met
3. Submit weekly certification
4. Record submission date

This routine prevents compliance drift.

---

## 6. Common Compliance Failures and Mitigations

### Missing a Week
- Set recurring reminders
- Certify even if activities were minimal

### Vague Log Entries
- Always document who, what, how, and result

### Inconsistent Records
- Ensure logs, certifications, and evidence align

### Refusing Suitable Work
- Document why the work was unsuitable
- Never ignore or ghost an opportunity

---

## 7. Audits, Investigations, and Overpayments

### 7.1 Audits
Audits are routine and do not imply wrongdoing.

You may be asked to provide:
- Work search logs
- Supporting evidence
- Clarifications

Respond promptly and professionally.

---

### 7.2 Overpayment Determinations
Overpayments often result from:
- Incorrect certifications
- Missing documentation
- Misunderstood income rules

Clear, contemporaneous records are your primary defense.

---

### 7.3 Appeals
If you appeal a determination:
- Your documentation becomes formal evidence
- Clear logs significantly improve outcomes

---

## 8. Compliance Mindset

Treat unemployment compliance as:
- Regulatory reporting, not journaling
- Defensive documentation, not narrative
- A disciplined process, not bureaucracy

Assume anything you record may be reviewed later.

---

## 9. Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Know your weekly activity requirement
- [ ] Log activities daily
- [ ] Keep entries specific and verifiable
- [ ] Submit weekly certifications on time
- [ ] Retain records for at least 12 months
- [ ] Maintain consistency across all records

---

## 10. Closing Guidance

If you:
- Perform legitimate work search activities
- Document them clearly
- Certify honestly and on time

You will remain compliant with TWC and minimize the risk of delays, denials, or overpayment determinations.

This document should be reviewed periodically and updated if TWC requirements or your circumstances change.
